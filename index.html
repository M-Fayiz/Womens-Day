About Malala

From Swat Valley to Global Icon

Born on July 12, 1997, in Mingora, Pakistan, Malala Yousafzai has become one of the world's most prominent advocates for girls' education and women's rights.

At age 11, she began writing a blog for the BBC about living under the Taliban's threats to deny girls education. In October 2012, she was targeted by the Taliban and shot in the head on her way home from school.

She survived and has continued her fight for education, becoming the youngest person ever to receive the Nobel Peace Prize in 2014 at the age of 17.

Malala graduated from Oxford University in 2020 with a degree in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics, continuing her advocacy through the Malala Fund, which she co-founded with her father, Ziauddin Yousafzai.

"We realize the importance of our voices only when we are silenced."

Began writing for BBC Urdu under the pseudonym "Gul Makai" about life under Taliban rule in Swat Valley.

New York Times Documentary

2010

Featured in a New York Times documentary, bringing international attention to her advocacy.

Survived Assassination Attempt

2012

Survived a targeted attack by Taliban gunmen while returning home from school.

Malala Fund Founded

2013

Co-founded the Malala Fund with her father to ensure 12 years of free, safe, quality education for all girls.

Nobel Peace Prize

2014

Became the youngest Nobel Prize laureate, receiving the Nobel Peace Prize at age 17.

Oxford University

2020

Graduated from Oxford University with a degree in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics.

Girls' Education
